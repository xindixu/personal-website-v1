Visualize HVAC Market Supply vs. Demand

Project Description

This is my individual project over the summer in Daikin North America as a Digital Marketing Intern. The goal of the project is helping users to identify which area in North America has an insufficient supply of HVAC distributors or dealers so that the company can better make decisions on opening up new branches, signing contracts with existing distributors or more emphasis on area marketing efforts to dealers.

There are three categories of data included: demand, supply, and performance, see “Data” section for more information. Users can toggle filters to narrow down to specific data range, as well as highlight a certain value in a specific field. Users can also collapse tabs to switch views among state, city and zip code.

Background

Data

Values

Demand: (from US census bureau)
  • Household population [HH#]
  • Percent of households using electricity as a source of home heating/cooling fuel [%Electricity]
  • Percent of the houses built in 2000-2009 (10-20 years old) [%10-20]
Supply: (from companies’ websites)
  • Company the location belongs to
  • Category (COD, IND, Competitors’ COD, Distributors not carrying our brand)
  • Brands the location is carrying
  • Address of the location
  • Total number of distributors in an area [D#]
Performance: (from Goodman database)
  • Goodman market share
  • Goodman unit sales from COD & IND (Jul. 2017 – Jun. 2018)

Geographic Levels

Demand:

All: State – City – ZIP Code

Supply:

All: State – City – ZIP Code

Performance:

Market share: Division – State – Trade Area – County – ZIP Code

Sales: State – Trade Area – County – ZIP Code

* Recommended/Default level is shown as Bold
