Texas Capitol

I made this architecture model during the first half of 2017 and gave to my ex-boyfriend as a gift. This is the 3rd architecture model I made, and it is totally different from my previous ones.

This model follows the design of the actual Texas Capitol. I found floor plans on their official website, as well as an online model from SketchUp. It can be separated into different parts: 1st, 2nd, 3rd floors, foyer, special rooms, roof, and dome. I put a lot of effort into making it as real as possible.

All the materials are hand-cut. The beige outside walls are made of museum boards, inner walls cardboards. Windows on the wall and the dome are made of plastic sheets. I originally planned to use poly foam balls for dome but ended up with just cutting, bending and sticking the boards to form a half sphere. (And I painted those foam balls into eyeballs for Halloween.) I also used balsa wood sheets for decoration. For the orders in front, I used bamboo sticks.
